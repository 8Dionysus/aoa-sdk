# Public Support CI Posture Contract

## Allowed Outputs

- Public support posture.
- Release claim boundaries.
- CI tier maps.
- Release-audit route references.
- Sibling canary matrix and compatibility drift report output.

## Stop-Lines

- Do not turn support posture into product-edge support guarantees.
- Do not claim sibling-owned meaning from SDK docs.
- Do not treat CI configuration as release publication truth.
- Do not treat a sibling canary pass as sibling release approval.
- Do not hide runtime, memory, proof, routing, role, or playbook authority in
  public support prose.

## Owner Split

The SDK owns what it can publicly claim about its control-plane helper role.
Sibling repositories own source meaning, and GitHub/package publication
surfaces own actual release state.
