# Agon Roadmap

Agon is the SDK control-plane face for center Agon pressure. It prepares
candidate helpers, review packets, and generated read models while the center
and owner repositories keep real Agon law, verdicts, rank, retention, and
Tree-of-Sophia boundaries.

## Current Contour

- Keep Agon helper families candidate-only unless an owner surface names the
  executable meaning.
- Keep center-law previews, state-packet review bindings, recurrence adapters,
  duel-kernel bindings, verdict/retention/rank helpers, epistemic/KAG helpers,
  school-lineage helpers, and Sophian-threshold helpers routed through active
  `parts/`.
- Keep generated candidate registries weaker than the source records,
  builders, and validation routes that create them.
- Keep every helper explicit about source owner, review mode, stop lines, and
  what the SDK is only preparing for inspection.

## Next Work

- Tighten helper contracts where an owner route, generated builder, or
  validation lane becomes stable enough to reduce ambiguity.
- Keep recurrence prebinding visible as review support, not scheduler behavior.
- Carry repeated Agon pressure into the owning part instead of opening a new
  package or broad SDK lane.

## When Time Comes

- Add a new Agon part when repeated helper pressure cannot stay in the current
  helper families without making the active route less readable.
- Add richer generated companions when source records and tests can prove they
  are read models, not Agon authority.
- Move a helper closer to execution only after the Agon owner names execution,
  rollback, review, and acceptance posture.

## Out Of Scope

- Live arena sessions.
- Verdict, rank, retention, scar, or ToS authority.
- Hidden recurrence scheduling.
- Proof, memory, runtime, or owner acceptance.
