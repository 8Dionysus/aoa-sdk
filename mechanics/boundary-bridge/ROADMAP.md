# Boundary Bridge Roadmap

Boundary Bridge is the SDK control-plane route for crossing owner boundaries
without importing sibling meaning. It gives typed handles, gates, readers, and
handoff packets; it does not make the SDK the owner of what those surfaces
mean.

## Current Contour

- Keep typed facades, consumed-surface posture gates, skill runtime bridges,
  technique promotion readiness readers, and owner-layer signal handoff routed
  through active `parts/`.
- Keep consumed sibling paths explicit and compatibility-checked before new
  readers become normal routes.
- Keep skill runtime reporting below `aoa-skills` meaning and host truth.
- Keep owner-layer signal detection additive, inspectable, and weaker than
  owner acceptance.

## Next Work

- Tighten compatibility maps where sibling paths or schemas have become stable
  enough to fail closed.
- Keep bridge helpers paired with source owner, route card, and focused tests.
- Move repeated cross-owner pressure into the nearest current part before
  considering a new bridge part.

## When Time Comes

- Add a new bridge part when repeated cross-owner pressure cannot stay inside
  compatibility, skill bridge, technique readiness, or owner-layer handoff.
- Widen facade behavior only after sibling source paths, fail-closed drift
  checks, and owner stop lines stay stable.
- Add generated bridge companions only when their builders and source routes are
  stronger than the companion.

## Out Of Scope

- Sibling source truth.
- Routing policy authority.
- Skill execution meaning.
- Proof, memory, role, stats, KAG, playbook, or runtime ownership.
- Owner acceptance without owner receipt.
